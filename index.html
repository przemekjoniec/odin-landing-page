What is a nebula?

Nebula is a cloud of interstellar gas and dust visible as a glowing patch or dark mist in the sky

In outer space, there are invisible clouds of gas detectable only through spectral analysis in invisible ranges.

Nebulae, including molecular clouds, are mainly composed of hydrogen and carbon monoxide, creating environments for new star formation.

Clouds of gas and dust in nebulae, such as the Orion Nebula, influence the size and lifespan of forming stars.
